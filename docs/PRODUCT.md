# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three overlapping audiences, each landing on the site with slightly different intentions:

1. **Potential employers and clients** — evaluating whether Filat is the right hire or contractor. They scan for taste level within the first few seconds; the design itself is the first deliverable they judge.
2. **Fellow developers and designers** — curious peers checking out work, stack choices, and creative direction. They read deeper and appreciate technical precision alongside visual sensibility.
3. **Casual referrals** — someone sent a link, they don't know what to expect. The site must orient them instantly without over-explaining.

In every case the first question is unspoken: *does this person have taste?* The answer must be self-evident before a word is read.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Filat Astakhov, a frontend developer who works across both engineering and design. The site exists to attract meaningful work — freelance contracts, full-time roles, collaborations — by demonstrating that technical skill and aesthetic judgment are inseparable in how he builds.

Success: a visitor closes the tab knowing exactly what kind of work Filat does, feeling confident in his quality, and having a clear path to reach out.

## Brand Personality

**Precise. Experienced. Plastic.**

- *Precise*: nothing is vague or approximate — choices are deliberate, edges are clean, copy says what it means.
- *Experienced*: confidence without boasting; the work speaks first.
- *Plastic*: capable of adapting form without losing identity — as comfortable in austere grids as in expressive layouts.

Voice: direct and dry. No superlatives, no self-congratulation, no filler phrases. One strong sentence beats three weak ones.

## Anti-references

- Purple-gradient-on-white developer portfolios that look like every other portfolio generated by AI.
- Heavy intro animations that make the user wait before seeing any content.
- "Cards grid" layouts where every project is a uniform rectangle with icon plus text — identical, forgettable.
- Glassmorphism used decoratively to signal "modern" without purpose.
- Any design where the visitor could guess the domain ("developer portfolio") before they register any specific personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Taste is the argument.** The design is credential zero. Before any project thumbnail or bio paragraph, the visual language must signal that this person builds things that look right.

2. **Artist-engineer, not either-or.** The work spans frontend code, UI engineering, and visual design. The site must make both readable simultaneously — not code-first with a design section bolted on, not a design portfolio that downplays the technical depth.

3. **Removal is the primary editorial act.** Every element earns its place by doing work nothing else can do. When in doubt, cut.

4. **Dark by intention, not convention.** The dark theme is atmosphere — warm, considered, with depth. Not "dark because developer tools are dark." The darkness should feel like a choice a photographer or architect would make, not a reflex.

5. **Every screen is a first screen.** Mobile visitors are not a secondary audience. Layout, hierarchy, and interaction must be designed for the smallest viewport first, then extended — never squeezed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Dark theme as the primary and only theme (deliberate product decision, not a shortcut).
- Maintain WCAG AA contrast ratios throughout — dark backgrounds demand careful foreground calibration.
- Reduced motion: all entrance and scroll animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Touch targets minimum 44×44px on mobile.
- No information conveyed by color alone.
